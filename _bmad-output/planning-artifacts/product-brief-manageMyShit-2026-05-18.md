# Product Brief: manageMyShit

## Executive Summary

manageMyShit is an uncompromising, privacy-first personal assistant designed to protect the user's health ("biological truths") and flow state ("sacred space") simultaneously. It acts as an aggressive, context-aware gatekeeper against interruptions while ensuring critical health prerequisites (medications, hydration) are met before complex architectural work can begin. By balancing deep work protection with an "Active Overwatch" system—including low-friction hourly health checks and multi-sensory nudges—the system prevents burnout without compromising productivity. Crucially, the system must remain effortless to use; its primary failure condition is becoming more complex to manage than the chaos it solves.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Deep, complex architectural work requires extended (4-hour minimum) blocks of uninterrupted focus. However, this "focus blindness" leads to skipping critical health routines (meals, medication for chronic conditions) and invites exhaustion, brain fog, and physical pain. The user is forced into a false dichotomy: neglect their health to achieve a critical "win," or constantly break their flow to handle mundane tasks and interruptions.

### Problem Impact

When flow is broken by irrelevant interruptions, productivity plummets, and morale drops. Conversely, when health routines are ignored due to hyper-focus, the biological toll is catastrophic, exacerbating chronic conditions and completely depleting the user's "Will." The lack of a system that respects both the heavy mental load of software architecture and the fragile limits of human biology leads to unsustainable work cycles.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Existing task managers and calendars are "bossy" and "rude." They utilize aggressive red text and snoozable alarms that feel insulting, breaking concentration without offering real assistance. They treat all tasks as a disorganized "pile of rocks" rather than organizing them into a tactical hierarchy. They entirely lack the intelligence to correlate biometric readiness with workload capacity, and they fail to enforce meaningful boundaries against outside noise.

### Proposed Solution

A holistic, mobile-anchored local AI assistant that operates concurrently on three strict pillars: Gatekeeping, Flow Protection, and Biological Reset. 
- It enforces a "Gentle Knock" notification system (ambient music fade-in -> screen dimming -> voice announcement) to respect mental loading.
- It features a rigid Gatekeeper that whitelists only immediate family and true emergencies. 
- It locks work timers until health routines are verified. 
- **Active Overwatch:** During deep flow, it performs non-intrusive hourly check-ins ("Throughput is down, are you ok?") that require no response unless the user is struggling, allowing for an immediate pivot to rest protocols.
Interactions are low-friction (e.g., capturing ideas via voice memo), and unfinished tasks are gracefully auto-sorted for approval during designated 'Attention Windows'. In "Crisis Mode," work rules can flex, but health tracking remains non-negotiable.

### Key Differentiators

- **Health-First Locking:** Work blocks cannot commence until biometric baselines (medications, hydration) are met, acting as a mandatory "Pre-flight" check.
- **The "Gentle Knock" & "Active Overwatch":** Multi-sensory fade-ins respect the user's mental state, while non-demanding hourly check-ins monitor throughput. A 2-hour "Final Fight" hard-stop prevents total biological exhaustion.
- **Strict, Respectful Boundaries:** Outside of designated 'Attention Windows', the AI does not interrupt unless a Gatekeeper emergency is triggered or throughput actively crashes. 
- **Zero Management Overhead:** The tool actively curates the backlog, handles the "Remains of the Day", and requires minimal setup/maintenance to run its concurrent systems. It is an assistant, not a manager.
